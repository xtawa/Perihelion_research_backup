## Abstract

When you already know a relationship is not realistically viable but still keep expecting a turnaround, the issue is usually not simple "willpower." It is often maintained by three interacting systems: uncertainty-driven reward seeking, attachment-based safety seeking, and sunk-cost reasoning.

A practical approach is not to argue with thoughts all day, but to dismantle the loop behaviorally: reduce triggers, limit checking, build replacement rewards, and re-invest in controllable goals.

This report provides an execution-first framework:
1. Assess involvement intensity and reality feasibility.
2. Run a three-phase intervention.
3. Track weekly metrics and adjust the plan.

## 1. Why You Keep Expecting the Impossible

### 1.1 Four Concepts to Separate

- Expectation: a forecast that "they will respond/change."
- Attachment: a safety-seeking system activated under stress.
- Hope: goal-directed agency and pathway thinking.
- Fantasy: emotional compensation through imagination.

In practice, what needs reducing is usually not hope itself, but the high-intensity pair of expectation plus checking behaviors.

### 1.2 The Typical Loop

1. Trigger: social media, delayed replies, accidental encounters, loneliness, stress.
2. Interpretation: over-reading signals, inflating possibility.
3. Emotion: anxiety, craving, grief.
4. Behavior: checking, messaging, rumination, mental rehearsal.
5. Short-term result: temporary relief.
6. Long-term result: stronger dependency on checking.

Intervention target: break the loop, not suppress emotions.

### 2.2 Reality Feasibility Check (RPC, Short)

First check hard constraints (any one means very low feasibility):
- They clearly rejected or clearly declined progression.
- They are in a stable committed relationship with no intent to change.
- There are explicit ethical or institutional constraints.
- Core life goals are fundamentally incompatible.

Then check evidence quality: is there stable, consistent, two-way investment, not occasional signals.

## 3. Three-Phase Intervention

### 3.1 Phase 1 (Days 1-7): Stabilize and Stop Bleeding

- Physiological baseline: fixed sleep window, no trigger scrolling before bed.
- Checking budget: hard limits on check frequency and duration.
- Urge delay: delay every checking impulse by 10 minutes.
- SUDS tracking: score distress before and after delay.
- Replacement tasks: at least one body, one social, one achievement task daily.

Goal: reduce behavioral intensity first, not thought frequency immediately.

### 3.2 Phase 2 (Weeks 2-3): Boundaries and Environment Control

Choose one strategy:
1. No-contact: remove triggers, close channels, pre-write relapse reset steps.
2. Low-contact: task-only communication, fixed channel, fixed time windows.

Supporting rules:
- No ambiguous relationship negotiation loops.
- 2-minute post-contact review to correct interpretation bias.

Goal: starve the uncertainty-reward loop.

### 3.3 Phase 3 (Week 4+): Goal Rebuilding and Relapse Prevention

- Value reset: shift from being chosen to measurable self-growth.
- Multi-track goals: work/study, social ties, body/hobbies.
- Self-compassion practice: reduce shame and self-attack.
- Trigger plan card: explicit if-then actions for high-risk moments.

Goal: move this person from central mission to ordinary variable.

## 4. Four-Week Tracking Template

Track daily (0-10):
- D (distress)
- C (craving)
- B (checking behavior count)
- F (functional recovery)

Weekly review:
1. What triggered me most this week?
2. Which action worked best?
3. Which barrier should I add next week?

Adjustment rules:
- If `B` does not drop: increase environmental barriers first.
- If `B` drops but `C` stays high: common withdrawal window, continue delay + replacement.
- If `D` stays high and `F` stays low: escalate to professional support.

## 5. Copy-Paste Scripts

### 5.1 Boundary Message (No-Contact Case)

> I need to refocus on my own life. To avoid repeated emotional loops, I will pause non-essential contact. If anything practical is needed, please use a fixed channel. Thanks for understanding.

### 5.2 Low-Contact Response (Mandatory Contact Case)

> I am keeping communication strictly task-focused for now, so I will only respond to practical topics.

### 5.3 Self-Reset Line (At Trigger Moment)

> I am seeking certainty to reduce pain. It helps briefly but restarts the cycle. I will do a 10-minute delay and one replacement action first.

---

## 6. When to Escalate to Professional Help

Escalate quickly if any of these are present:
- Persistent sleep/work/study impairment for 2+ weeks.
- Repetitive checking behavior that you cannot interrupt.
- Continuous emotional worsening, hopelessness, or self-harm thoughts.

Self-help and professional support are complementary, not exclusive.
